<p><p>Onychomycosis, a fungal infection of the nails, is primarily treated using two types of medications: oral and topical drugs. Oral drugs, such as terbinafine and itraconazole, are systemic treatments that target the infection from within, providing a higher cure rate but potentially causing side effects. In contrast, topical drugs, including antifungal lacquers and creams, are applied directly to the infected nail, offering a more localized treatment with fewer side effects, but often with lower effectiveness compared to oral options.</p></p>
