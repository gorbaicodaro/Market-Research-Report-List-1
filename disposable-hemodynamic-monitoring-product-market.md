<p><p>The disposable hemodynamic monitoring product market is categorized into three main types: invasive, minimally invasive, and non-invasive. Invasive methods involve direct measurement through catheter insertion into the bloodstream, providing precise data but carrying higher risks. Minimally invasive approaches use less invasive techniques to gather information, balancing accuracy and reduced risk. Non-invasive monitoring eliminates the need for penetration, relying on external sensors to assess hemodynamics, making it safer but potentially less detailed in measurement accuracy. Each type serves specific clinical needs.</p></p>
